Rawa Dhokla Recipe

Description

Rawa dhokla is a popular snack from the western Indian state of Gujarat. It is made from rawa, or semolina, which is mixed with yogurt and spices to form a batter. The batter is then steamed in a special dhokla mold or steamer until it is cooked through. Rawa dhokla is typically served with a chutney or sauce on top, and it is often garnished with chopped coriander leaves. It is light, fluffy, and has a slightly sour taste due to the yogurt. Rawa dhokla is often eaten as a snack or as part of a meal.

Method

For Dhokla

  1. Take buttermilk in a bowl. Add rawa to it.
  2. Mix continuously as you add rawa, so that no lumps form. Now add salt to taste.
  3. Keep the batter at room temperature for 30 minutes.
  4. Add soda/eno to the batter when you are about to cook it.
  5. Take a cooker and fill 2 glasses of water in it and place a stand under it.
  6. Grease a container all over with ghee and pour the batter into it.
  7. Place the tin inside the cooker and steam it for 35-40 min at medium flame.
  8. Be sure to remove the whistle and gasket from the cooker.
  9. Check with a spoon or knife. If it comes out clean, it's ready... otherwise, give it more time.
  10. Take out the dhokla from the tin and cut it into square pieces.

For Tadka

  1. Add oil to a pan and heat it.
  2. Once the mustard seeds sizzle, add the curry leaves and green chillies.
  3. Now add a pinch of turmeric powder, red chilli powder and salt as per taste.
  4. Add the dhoklas and mix them well.
  5. Garnish it with corriander, green chilli and serve it with Tamarind chutney.
  6. Now your Rawa Dhoklas are ready to eat.

More thoughts

Rawa dhokla can be served as a snack or as part of a meal. It is typically served at room temperature or slightly warm. Here are a few serving suggestions:

  • Cut the dhokla into small squares or triangles and serve with a chutney or sauce on top. Some popular chutneys to serve with dhokla include coconut chutney, coriander chutney, and tamarind chutney.
  • Garnish the dhokla with chopped coriander leaves, grated coconut, and/or a sprinkle of chaat masala.
  • Serve the dhokla as part of a larger spread of snacks, such as pakoras, samosas, and bhajias.
  • Serve the dhokla as part of a thali, or traditional Indian platter, along with rice, dal, vegetables, and chapatis.
  • Enjoy the dhokla as a snack with a cup of chai or masala chai.
